## Finding 2: Efficient Cross-Encoder Reranking via Early Exit (SIGIR 2025)

**Paper:** [Efficient Re-ranking with Cross-encoders via Early Exit](https://dl.acm.org/doi/10.1145/3726302.3729962)
**Source type:** Peer-reviewed paper at SIGIR 2025 (top IR venue)
**Credibility:** 7 authors from known IR research groups; published at ACM SIGIR; open code at [github.com/veneres/SEE-SIGIR25](https://github.com/veneres/SEE-SIGIR25)
**Date:** July 2025 (SIGIR '25, Padua)

**Summary:** Cross-encoders are highly effective for reranking but computationally expensive because they process query-document pairs through every transformer layer. This paper proposes an early-exit strategy (SEE) that terminates inference at intermediate layers when confidence is sufficient, dramatically reducing latency while maintaining ranking quality. This is exactly what a CLI tool needs — fast reranking on CPU without sacrificing precision.

**Maps to:** Spec 2 — cross-encoder reranking stage of hybrid pipeline
**Actionable:**
- Study the SEE approach for BookLib's planned cross-encoder reranker in `src/retrieval/reranker.ts`
- The early-exit approach could make cross-encoder reranking viable even on modest hardware
- Evaluate FlashRank (lightweight cross-encoder lib) as a simpler alternative baseline
- Benchmark: early-exit cross-encoder vs. full cross-encoder vs. no reranking on BookLib's eval harness

**Impact:** HIGH | **Effort:** MEDIUM (requires implementing reranking stage from Spec 2)

**Credibility:** Working implementation with C#/Java/Python; BAAI's BGE-M3 is a well-established model
